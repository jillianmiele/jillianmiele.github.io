Overview

The Problem

College students are often disconnected with the communities surrounding their university. These same students find it quite difficult to secure an internship or job offer after graduation due to lack of experience.

Our Solution

Gigpool streamlines the experience of being a delivery driver/gig-economy worker by consolidating all of the gig-economy services one drives for into one centralized hub. Gigpool calculates the optimal route for the driver based on proximity and revenue, calculates the optimal route to take to save gas, sets one standardized radius for all pickups/deliveries, and combines finances/transaction history.

How might we streamline gig-economy delivery services to benefit the drivers' experiences?

Empathizing
with Drivers

Universities funnel students with great minds without much regard to the cities and communities surrounding them. As this happens, students generally follow a strict plan of completing classes while maybe working a part-time job or two, then getting internships (if they have proper experience) over the summer until graduation.  

Throughout this process, some students need to make some money on the side during their semester. The current solution is working a part-time job in the dining hall, being an office assistant, etc. What if there is a better way to combine the aspects of a part-time job with an experience that benefits the career path of students?

Empathy Map

Using the data from my contextual observation, user feedback collection, and available data, I was able to create an empathy map representing a hypothetical user who could benefit from my app and efficiently summarize their needs and goals. I used this persona when designing to ensure that my solution looped back to the needs and goals outlined in him.

Design Opportunity

Design

Problem:  Difficulty with scheduling. Dashers feel frustrated when they find no schedule available in their preferred time and location. Most dashers want to dash within their area zone.
Solution: Notify dashers when there’s possible time slots are available. Make it more convenient for dashers to filter locations where they would like to dash in.

Problem: Keeping up with earnings. The utmost priority for Dashers is to make more money. However, with the existing feature, it is hard for users to know how and when they could get paid more.
Solution: Show users when the peak time (can get extra pay) is for picking up orders.
Provide visual hierarchy to make it easier for drivers to analyze and learn about their earnings.

Problem: Want to feel accomplished. Dashers care a lot of about acceptance rate and consumer rating because those are the feedback they get on their job. They want to feel that their work is valuable and meaningful.
Solution: Give compliments or feedback that could provide an appreciation for their achievements. I want to make sure that dashers feel satisfied with what they are doing for their job.
