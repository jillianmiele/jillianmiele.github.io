OVERVIEW

Problem: Attention is currency. The more you pay attention to the apps on your phone, the more your attention is monetized. On average, we receive about 200 notifications per day, from texts and calls to social media apps and promotional e-mails. â€‹Every time you reach for your phone, it takes you over 23 minutes to get back into the "zone," according to Jeremy Boudinet, marketing manager at Nextiva. This affects the quality and the caliber of your work as well as your comfort and well-being as an individual.

Solution: Quiet Hours is an app I conceptualized and designed to hold your notifications and deliver them at a time that suits you. When you open your lock screen, you will not see any notifications except the time left for your batch to be delivered for you - whether you're on Do Not Disturb or not. Quiet Hours gives you the option to whitelist certain apps and contacts in case of emergency while streamlining your productivity and protecting you from invasive notifications. When you are finally ready to receive your scheduled notifications, you may review them entirely within the Quiet Hours app and schedule your next notification batch.

Competitive Analysis

My first step to designing Quiet Hours was to take a closer look at the existing solutions. I focused on two key players â€” the built-in Do Not Disturb app on the iPhone, and Notification Blocker & Cleaner & Heads-up Off on the App Store, which has over 20,000 users. I read dozens of app store reviews and Reddit forums while exploring these features myself to understand why people use these services and how I could leverage them to innovate Quiet Hours.

First, I researched user feedback on the Notification Blockers & Cleaner app. I scoured through many five-star reviews and noted what the app excelled at to garner such a high userbase and also looked through the one-star reviews to note what could be done better. By doing this, I was able to pinpoint common pain points that users might experience.

Then, I committed myself to using Apple's Do Not Disturb feature for a week to find the strengths and weaknesses in terms of personal comfort and productivity. I also asked my roommates and three of my friends to do the same and interviewed them after the work week to understand the various pain points of different users. Putting myself in this position and the contextual observations from my friends led me to truly empathize with those who utilize this service, and encouraged me to create actionable solutions that makes up for its weaknesses with Quiet Hours.

User Feedback

Relevant Findings: Users do not find the Notification Blockers & Cleaner app effective despite its high demand and userbase; have to keep app running in the background for it to work; low-quality product that needs constant maintenance and upkeep; does not give an option to whitelist certain contacts or apps; expensive subscription fee to clear ads; intrusive and anxiety-inducing ads if using free version

Design

Problem:  Difficulty with scheduling. Dashers feel frustrated when they find no schedule available in their preferred time and location. Most dashers want to dash within their area zone.
Solution: Notify dashers when thereâ€™s possible time slots are available. Make it more convenient for dashers to filter locations where they would like to dash in.

Problem: Keeping up with earnings. The utmost priority for Dashers is to make more money. However, with the existing feature, it is hard for users to know how and when they could get paid more.
Solution: Show users when the peak time (can get extra pay) is for picking up orders.
Provide visual hierarchy to make it easier for drivers to analyze and learn about their earnings.

Problem: Want to feel accomplished. Dashers care a lot of about acceptance rate and consumer rating because those are the feedback they get on their job. They want to feel that their work is valuable and meaningful.
Solution: Give compliments or feedback that could provide an appreciation for their achievements. I want to make sure that dashers feel satisfied with what they are doing for their job.
