I've always been an artist, if the doodles in the margins of all my class notes since elementary school could tell you anything. However, my user experience design journey didn't begin until my sophomore year of college when I was working as a classroom technology assistant. When I was helping my peers, I expected to be messing around with hardware and troubleshooting network issues. To my surprise, most of my job consisted of helping everyday people use poorly-designed apps, websites, and software. I was studying web design and couldn't help but feel frustrated about how simple it would be to pinpoint and fix these usability issues. I felt empathy for the users, and as soon as I discovered user experience design, I made it my mission to create inclusive and beautiful experiences that are easy to use. 

I'm a designer from a blended background: I get my analytical skills from my Applied Data Analytics education and research experience, meticulousness from my twenty years of drawing and painting, and scrappiness from my first-generation immigrant parents. I love working in the space where my mind and my hands meet.

I'm also a native New Yorker, originally hailing from the Bronx and now living in northern New Jersey. My identity has shaped me to cherish diversity of all kinds, and has proven the importance of community. I truly believe in the power of design to inspire and create change. Using empathy and curiosity to make informed ethical and equitable design choices can bring people together and promote human goodness as a whole--and I want to be at the forefront of it all.
